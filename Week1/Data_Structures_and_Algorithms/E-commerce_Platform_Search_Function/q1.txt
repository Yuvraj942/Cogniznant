Big O notation is a mathematical tool used in computer science to describe the
performance or complexity of an algorithm. Specifically, it describes the upper
bound of the algorithm's growth rate as the input size ($n$) increases to infinity.
It helps us answer the question: As our e-commerce catalog grows from 1,000 
products to 1,000,000 products, how much longer will the search take?

Best-Case Scenario: The target item is found immediately (e.g., it is the very first item we check).
This is usually O(1) or constant time.

Worst-Case Scenario: The target item is the very last one checked, or it doesn't exist in the database at all, 
forcing the algorithm to do the maximum amount of work.
This is the scenario Big O notation usually focuses on.
